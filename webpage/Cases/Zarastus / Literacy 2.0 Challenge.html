EdTech & Misinformation

Zarastus — Fighting Misinformation Through Education

As part of the Literacy 2.0 Challenge: Bridging Tradition and Tech in Helsinki, our team co-created Zarastus, a browser plug-in powered by Perplexity AI. The tool helps students and educators compare media sources, spot bias, and access credible information — building critical thinking skills against misinformation and hybrid threats in the age of AI.

Challenge

Literacy 2.0 — Why Zarastus matters

The Literacy 2.0 Challenge brought together innovators to tackle one of the most urgent issues online: misinformation. Adults report seeing false or misleading content every day, yet only a minority feel confident telling real news from fake. The event pushed us to bridge critical thinking, data privacy and branding into one practical solution for students and educators.

Context highlights:
  • Older adults report encountering false information online far more often than younger users, yet only a small share of people say they feel very confident separating real from fake news.
  • Mentors and judges emphasised the need for tools that don’t just fact-check, but actively teach media literacy and critical thinking.
  • Over three days we iterated quickly with support from HEH mentors, refining our concept from vague idea to a focused education-first plug-in.

Solution

Zarastus — Education aggregator & browser plug-in

Zarastus is a browser plug-in that works as an education aggregator. Powered by Perplexity AI, it guides users to compare media sources, surface different viewpoints and reflect on bias instead of passively accepting a single narrative.

Key features:
  • Source comparison: lets users place articles and posts side-by-side to understand how stories are framed across media.
  • Bias & credibility prompts: nudges readers to check author, evidence and possible agendas behind each source.
  • Classroom-ready flows: designed for students and educators to use in assignments, helping them practice critical reading in real time.
