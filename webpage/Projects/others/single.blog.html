Amplifying the Stage – FCU Musical Theatre

By Hazel Lin · June 19, 2019 · 5 min read

As long as I can remember, I’ve been drawn to the backstage – the quiet chaos that makes a show feel effortless to the audience.

Working with FCU Musical Theatre let me blend marketing, logistics, and experience design – from promotions to the moment the curtain fell.

My goal is to capture the energy you don’t see on stage.

Audience members remember the songs and the spotlight. But the emotional core of a show often happens in the wings: quick eye contact between cast members, last-minute mic checks, or the moment everyone takes a deep breath before the opening note.

For this production, I documented not only the performance, but also the timelines, checklists, and communications that held everything together. This post is part memory, part playbook for future student teams.

Every production starts long before opening night. For FCU Musical Theatre, it began with a simple question: What story do we want to tell, and who do we want to invite into it?

My role mixed marketing strategy with logistics. I helped translate creative ideas into a roadmap: campaign milestones, rehearsal schedules, content pieces, and internal updates that kept everyone aligned.

Promotion wasn’t just about posters. It meant understanding which channels students actually check, what would make them pause, and how to keep them engaged from first teaser to ticket purchase.

On show days, that work shifted into operations: checking call times, confirming cues with tech, and making sure performers had what they needed to focus on their roles instead of logistics.

“The magic of a show is never an accident – it’s the result of dozens of quiet decisions that no one ever sees.”

— Hazel
